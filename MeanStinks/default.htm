2.

  • A. Write a poem about it in your journal.
  • B. Share your feelings about it in a Facebook post.
  • C. Spread the word online about the disaster and try to get others to donate for relief efforts.
  • D. Ask your relatives for donations.
  • E. Convince a friend to take action together.
  • F. Feel badly about it but get distracted by a million other things as soon as the film is over.

3.

  • A. Caring and compassionate.
  • B. Practical and creative.
  • C. Outspoken and political.
  • D. Brave and direct.
  • E. Supportive and friendly.
  • F. Popular and observant.

4.

  • A. Listen to everyone's perspective and help make them feel better.
  • B. Get the warring parties to talk peacefully to each other.
  • C. Get someone to intervene.
  • D. Confront the person you believe is wrong and convince them to apologize.
  • E. Take one person's side in the conflict and support her wholeheartedly.
  • F. Stay out of it, even though you know what you'd do in the same situation.

5.

  • A. Complain about how stupid the person is to your friends.
  • B. Try to convince your friends to write a letter to the editor.
  • C. Report the person who did it to a teacher.
  • D. Confront the alarm puller after school and tell him how stupid his prank was.
  • E. Help a friend compose an email to the guy telling him how stupid his prank was, but you don't sign your name.
  • F. Laugh about it with your friends but secretly think it was a bad idea.

6.

  • A. Drop everything to listen to your friend, but refuse to get involved.

  • D. Tell your friend you think it's an unfair fight and ask her to let it go.
  • E. Vent to a third girl about how wrong your friend is being.
  • F. Feel badly for the other girl, but join your friend's side 100%.

  • C. I wish more adults got involved and cared.
  • D. I would love to tell the queen bee at this school exactly what I think.
  • E. I would do something if other people would.
  • F. It's awful, but what's the point of doing anything? There will always be drama.

8.

  • A. Ask your friend why she did it.
  • B. Apologize to the other girl on behalf of your friend.
  • C. Tell your school counselor.
  • D. Tell your friend to stop, and that being mean stinks.
  • E. Try to convince another girl to say something to your friend.
  • F. It's just one text. She'll get over it.

  • B. Being creative.
  • C. Connecting with people of all ages.
  • D. Talking in front of lots of people.
  • E. Cheering up friends.
  • F. Making new friends.

10.

  • A. Talk to my BFF.
  • B. Write in my journal.
  • C. Talk to my mom.
  • D. Talk to the person who is making me upset.
  • E. Do something that makes me happy, like eating a piece of chocolate or going for a walk.
  • F. Spend time with friends.

11.

  • B. Wise owl.
  • C. Talkative parrot.
  • D. Brave lion.
  • E. Energetic puppy.
  • F. Color-changing chameleon.

12.

  • A. Do the work for the group member who screws up or forgets.
  • B. Delegate responsibilities to other members.
  • C. Talk to a teacher if people aren’t working together well.
  • D. Let slackers know when they’re not pulling their weight.
  • E. Make sure everyone is doing what they’re supposed to.
  • F. Do my job and hope everyone does theirs.
